Script 1: The Perfect Greeting

Never Say: "Thank you for calling [Business], how can I help you?"
This is passive and puts the caller in control.
YOUR OPENING
"Good [morning/afternoon], thank you for calling [Business Name]. This is [Your Name]. I'm so glad you called today - are you calling for yourself or someone else?"
WHY IT WORKS

Establishes warmth, takes control with a question, and qualifies if they're the decision-maker.

Script 2: The Price Question

CALLER SAYS
"How much do you charge for [service]?"
YOUR RESPONSE
"Great question! I want to make sure I give you accurate information."

"Our [service] pricing depends on several factors that [Expert Name] evaluates during your consultation - things like [factor 1], [factor 2], and your specific situation."

"Generally, [service] in our practice ranges from $[low] to $[high]. But the best way to get your exact price is a consultation where [Expert Name] can assess your specific needs."

"Speaking of which - we have a consultation opening on [day] at [time]. Would that work for your schedule, or is there another time that's better?"
KEY TECHNIQUE

Give a range, then IMMEDIATELY redirect to booking. Don't wait for a response about price.

IF THEY PUSH BACK
"I'm just calling around to get prices. Can you just give me a number?"
YOUR RESPONSE
"I completely understand wanting to do your research - that's smart."

"Here's something important to know: price alone doesn't tell the whole story. A cheaper option with poor results can end up costing much more in the long run."

"[Expert Name] has been doing this for [X] years with excellent results. Our consultations are [free/$X] and there's no pressure - it's about giving you the information you need to make the right decision."

"Would you like me to get you scheduled? Even if you're comparing options, you'll want to meet the expert before making this kind of decision."

Script 3: "Let Me Think About It"

CALLER SAYS
"I need to think about it. I'll call back."
YOUR RESPONSE
"Of course! This is a big decision and you should feel confident."

"Before you go, can I ask - what's holding you back from booking a consultation today? I might be able to help address any concerns."

[Listen to their response]

"I understand. Many of our clients felt the same way before their consultation."

"What I can do is send you some information about [their concern] - would you prefer email or text?"

[Get their contact info]

"Perfect. I'll send that over right now. And just so you know - we do book up [X] weeks in advance. If you decide you want to move forward, I'd hate for you to have to wait. Can I pencil you in for a consultation and you can always reschedule if needed?"
THE PSYCHOLOGY

You've now: (1) captured their contact info, (2) created urgency, (3) made booking feel low-commitment. 70% of these convert when you follow up.

Script 4: Follow-Up Messages

Same-Day Follow-Up Text:

Hi [Name]! This is [Your Name] from [Business]. It was great chatting with you today about [topic].

As promised, here's the info on [what they asked about]: [link]

Ready to book your consultation? Just reply and I'll get you scheduled. We have openings this week!

3-Day Follow-Up:

Hi [Name] - just checking in! Did you have a chance to look at the info I sent?

I know [topic] is a big decision. If you have any questions at all, I'm here to help.

Would next [Tuesday/Wednesday] work for a consultation? I have a 10am or 2pm available.
