Phase 1: The Opening (First 5 Minutes)

Setting the Frame

YOUR OPENING
"Thank you for taking the time to meet with me today, [Name]. Before we dive in, I want to make sure we use this time wisely."

"My goal for our conversation is to understand your situation completely, answer any questions you have, and if it makes sense for both of us, discuss how I can help."

"If at the end of our conversation, we're not a fit - that's perfectly fine. I'll point you in the right direction regardless."

"Does that sound fair?"
WHY IT WORKS

This removes pressure, establishes authority, and positions you as selective - not desperate.

Phase 2: Discovery (Minutes 5-20)

Understanding Their Situation

DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
"Tell me what's going on. What brought you here today?"

[Let them talk - take notes]

"How long has this been going on?"

"What have you tried so far to solve this?"

"What happens if this doesn't get resolved?"

"In an ideal world, what would the perfect outcome look like for you?"

Uncovering the Real Stakes

GOING DEEPER
"Beyond the immediate issue, what else is this affecting in your life?"

"How is this impacting your [family/business/peace of mind]?"

"What would it mean to you to have this resolved?"
KEY INSIGHT

People don't pay for services - they pay for outcomes and relief from pain. Uncover the emotional stakes.

Phase 3: The Presentation (Minutes 20-35)

Presenting Your Solution

THE BRIDGE
"Based on everything you've told me, here's what I see..."

"[Summarize their situation - show you listened]"

"I've helped [number] of clients in similar situations, and here's the approach that works:"

"[Explain your process in 3-4 clear steps]"

"The outcome we're working toward is [their stated ideal outcome]."

Introducing Investment

THE INVESTMENT CONVERSATION
"For a case like yours, my retainer is [amount]."

"[PAUSE - let them process]"

"This covers [what's included]. You'll have [benefits/access/timeline]."

"What questions do you have about how this works?"
CRITICAL

State the price confidently and STOP TALKING. The first person to speak after the price loses.

Phase 4: Objection Handling

OBJECTION: "I need to think about it"
"I need to think about it and get back to you."
YOUR RESPONSE
"I completely understand. This is a significant decision."

"Can I ask - what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the approach, the investment, or something else?"

[Address their specific concern]

"What would help you make a confident decision today?"
OBJECTION: "It's too expensive"
"That's more than I was expecting. I'm not sure I can afford that."
YOUR RESPONSE
"I appreciate you being honest with me."

"Let me ask you this: What's the cost of NOT solving this problem? You mentioned earlier that [reference their pain point]."

"If this doesn't get resolved, what does that cost you over the next [timeframe]?"

"We also have payment options that can make this more manageable. Would you like to hear about those?"
OBJECTION: "I need to talk to my spouse/partner"
"I need to discuss this with my [spouse/partner] first."
YOUR RESPONSE
"Of course - this is a decision that affects both of you."

"What do you think their main concerns will be?"

[Address those concerns]

"Would it be helpful if I scheduled a brief call with both of you so I can answer their questions directly?"

Phase 5: The Close

The Assumptive Close

CLOSING SCRIPT
"Based on our conversation, I'm confident I can help you achieve [their goal]."

"Here's what happens next: We'll sign the agreement today, and I'll [first step you'll take]."

"Would you prefer to put this on a card, or would a bank transfer work better for you?"
