Part 1: Capture

The Perfect Before/After Photo Setup

1

Lighting Setup

Consistent lighting is everything. Inconsistent lighting = accusations of editing.

  • Use a dedicated photo room with controlled lighting
  • Two soft boxes at 45¬∞ angles, 5500K daylight bulbs
  • No windows - artificial light only for consistency
  • Same lighting position for EVERY patient
2

Camera Position & Settings

Mark the floor. Same distance, same angle, every time.

  • Tripod at fixed height (mark the wall for reference)
  • Patient stands on floor markers
  • Camera 5-8 feet from patient depending on procedure
  • Use same lens (50mm or 85mm recommended)
  • Aperture: f/8-f/11 for sharpness
3

Patient Positioning

Capture all relevant angles for each procedure type.

Face Procedures:
  • Front facing, neutral expression
  • 45¬∞ left and right
  • Full profile left and right
  • Bird's eye (looking down)
  • Worm's eye (looking up)
Body Procedures:
  • Front facing, arms at sides
  • 45¬∞ left and right
  • Full profile left and right
  • Back view
‚ö†Ô∏è Critical: Take BEFORE photos in pre-op, not on surgery day. Patients look different when nervous/fasting. Take AFTER photos at consistent intervals (1 month, 3 months, 6 months, 1 year).
Part 2: Edit

Ethical Editing for Social Media

üéØ The Golden Rule: You can adjust lighting, color, and crop. You CANNOT alter anatomy. Smoothing skin or changing body proportions = unethical and can lead to lawsuits.

Allowed Edits:

  • Match white balance between before/after
  • Adjust exposure to look consistent
  • Crop to center the subject
  • Remove background distractions
  • Add your watermark/logo
  • Add text overlays (procedure name, time elapsed)

Recommended Apps & Tools:

Lightroom Mobile

Best for batch editing with presets. Create a "Before/After" preset once, apply to all photos.

Canva Pro

Perfect for adding text, creating side-by-side layouts, adding your brand elements.

TouchRetouch

Remove bra straps, surgical marks, or background objects (NOT body features).

VSCO

Premium filters that maintain skin tones naturally. Use same filter on both images.

Part 3: Compliance

HIPAA-Safe Sharing System

‚ö†Ô∏è Legal Warning: Sharing patient photos without proper consent is a HIPAA violation that can result in fines up to $50,000 per incident. Always get written consent.

The Consent Process:

1

Timing

Get consent AFTER surgery, during a follow-up visit when they're happy with results. Never ask before - they might feel pressured.

2

The Consent Form Must Include:

  • Specific platforms where photos may be used
  • Whether face will be shown or cropped
  • Duration of consent (forever or specific period)
  • Right to revoke consent at any time
  • Patient signature and date
3

How to Ask (Script)

"Your results are absolutely beautiful, and I think other people considering [procedure] would really benefit from seeing what's possible. Would you be open to letting us share your before/after photos? We can either show them anonymously - just the procedure area - or with your permission, include your face. Either way, you'd be helping someone who feels the same way you did before surgery."
