Part 1

The Perfect Consultation Opening

The first 2 minutes set the tone. Here's how to start with connection, not clinical assessment.

YOU SAY
"Before we get into any medical stuff, I want to understand YOU. What made you finally decide to come in today? I'm curious - what was the moment that made you think, 'okay, I'm actually going to do this?'"
üí° Why This Works: This question reveals their emotional motivation - the REAL reason they're there. It's not about the procedure. It's about how they feel when they look in the mirror.
WHEN THEY ANSWER
Listen for:
  • "I've hated this for years..."
  • "Every time I see photos..."
  • "My confidence has been..."
  • "After my divorce/weight loss/pregnancy..."
RESPOND WITH
"I completely understand. And I want you to know - feeling that way is valid. This isn't vanity. This is about feeling like yourself again. Let me show you what's possible..."
Part 2

The Top 5 Objections (And How to Handle Each)

OBJECTION #1

"I need to think about it"

What they're really saying: "I'm scared and need reassurance."
YOUR RESPONSE
"Absolutely - this is a big decision and you should feel 100% confident. Can I ask what specifically you want to think through? Is it the procedure itself, the timing, the investment, or something else? I want to make sure I've given you all the information you need."
üí° Pro Tip: This opens the door to address their REAL concern, which is usually fear of the unknown or cost.
OBJECTION #2

"It's too expensive" / "I can't afford it"

What they're really saying: "I'm not sure it's worth the investment."
YOUR RESPONSE
"I hear you. And I never want finances to be the reason someone doesn't feel confident in their own skin. Let me ask you this - if cost wasn't a factor at all, would you move forward today?"

[If they say yes]

"Good. Then let's talk about making this work. We have financing options that break this into monthly payments of around $[X]. When you think about what you spend monthly on things that don't give you lasting results - dinners out, clothes you don't love, skincare that doesn't work - this is an investment in how you feel every single day for the rest of your life."
OBJECTION #3

"I need to talk to my husband/partner"

YOUR RESPONSE
"Of course - it's important to be on the same page with your partner. Is their concern about the procedure itself, or more about the investment?"

[Then]

"Here's something I've found: often partners are concerned because they don't fully understand what's involved. Would it help if I gave you some materials to share with them, or even had a brief call to answer their questions? I want them to feel as confident as you do."
üí° Alternative: "Many of my patients say their partners end up being their biggest supporters once they see the results. Would your partner be supportive if they knew how much this meant to you?"
OBJECTION #4

"I'm scared of surgery / What if something goes wrong?"

YOUR RESPONSE
"That fear is completely normal - and honestly, I'd be worried if you weren't taking this seriously. Here's what I want you to know: I've performed over [X,000] procedures. My complication rate is less than [X]%. We operate in a fully accredited facility with a board-certified anesthesiologist. But more importantly, I want YOU to feel safe."

"What would help you feel more comfortable? Would you like to speak with a past patient? See more before/afters? Tour our operating facility? I'll do whatever it takes for you to feel confident."
OBJECTION #5

"I found someone cheaper" / "I'm getting quotes from other surgeons"

YOUR RESPONSE
"That's smart - you should do your research. Can I share something with you though?"

"In plastic surgery, you're not buying a product that's the same everywhere. You're buying my hands, my experience, my artistic eye, and my judgment. I've spent [X] years and trained with [credentials] to develop those skills."

"There's a reason people fly from [other cities/countries] to have me do their surgery. And I'll tell you honestly - I see revision patients every week who went somewhere cheaper first. They end up paying twice."

"I'm not the cheapest. But I am absolutely committed to giving you a result you'll love for life. That's worth the difference."
Part 3

The Close: Getting to "Yes" Naturally

THE SOFT CLOSE
"Based on everything we've discussed, I think [procedure] would give you exactly what you're looking for. I can see you [achieving their stated goal - looking refreshed, feeling confident, etc.]. Do you see it too?"

[Wait for their response - usually enthusiastic yes]

"Great. Then let's make it happen. My next available surgery dates are [X] and [Y]. Which works better for your schedule?"
üí° Key: Don't ask "Do you want to proceed?" Ask "Which date works for you?" Assume the close.
THE URGENCY CLOSE (When Needed)
"I want to be transparent with you - my schedule fills up [X] weeks in advance, and I'm already booking into [month]. If you want to have this done by [their goal - summer/wedding/event], we'd need to schedule soon."

"I'm not trying to pressure you. But I've seen too many patients wait and then be disappointed when they can't get on the calendar when they want. Would it make sense to at least secure a date? You can always move it if needed."
