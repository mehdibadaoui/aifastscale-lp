Script 1

The Perfect Greeting

‚ùå Never Say: "Thank you for calling Dr. Smith's office, how can I help you?"
This is passive. It puts the caller in control and leads to price shopping.
YOUR OPENING
"Good [morning/afternoon], thank you for calling [Practice Name]. This is [Your Name]. I'm so glad you called today - are you calling about a procedure for yourself?"
WHY IT WORKS

This immediately establishes warmth ("so glad you called") and takes control of the conversation with a question. It also assumes positive intent - they're interested in becoming a patient.

Script 2

The Price Question (Make or Break Moment)

CALLER SAYS
"How much is a [rhinoplasty/facelift/tummy tuck]?"
YOUR RESPONSE
"Great question! I want to make sure I give you accurate information. Our [procedure] pricing varies based on several factors that Dr. [Name] evaluates during your consultation - things like the specific techniques needed, anesthesia time, and your individual anatomy."

"Generally, [procedure] in our practice ranges from $[low] to $[high]. But the best way to get your exact price is a consultation where Dr. [Name] can assess your specific goals."

"Speaking of which - we have a consultation opening on [day] at [time]. Would that work for your schedule, or is there another time that's better?"
KEY TECHNIQUE

Give a range, then IMMEDIATELY redirect to booking. Don't wait for a response about price - assume the booking question.

IF THEY PUSH BACK
"I'm just calling around to get prices. Can you just give me a number?"
YOUR RESPONSE
"I completely understand wanting to do your research - that's smart. Here's something important to know: in plastic surgery, price alone doesn't tell the whole story. A $3,000 rhinoplasty with complications can end up costing $15,000 in revisions."

"Dr. [Name] has been performing [procedure] for [X] years with a [low] complication rate. Our consultations are [free/$X] and there's no pressure - it's about giving you the information you need to make the right decision."

"Would you like me to get you scheduled? Even if you're comparing options, you'll want to meet the surgeon before making this kind of decision."
Script 3

The "Let Me Think About It" Objection

CALLER SAYS
"I need to think about it. I'll call back."
YOUR RESPONSE
"Of course! This is a big decision and you should feel confident. Before you go, can I ask - what's holding you back from booking a consultation today? I might be able to help address any concerns."

[Listen to their response - it's usually fear, cost, or timing]

"I understand. Many of our patients felt the same way before their consultation. What I can do is send you some information about [their concern] - would you prefer email or text?"

[Get their contact info]

"Perfect. I'll send that over right now. And just so you know - we do book up [X] weeks in advance. If you decide you want to move forward, I'd hate for you to have to wait. Can I pencil you in for a consultation and you can always reschedule if needed?"
THE PSYCHOLOGY

You've now: (1) captured their contact info, (2) created urgency with booking timeline, (3) made booking feel low-commitment with "pencil in." 70% of these convert when you follow up.
