The "I Can't Afford It" Reality

When patients say "I can't afford it," what they usually mean is:

"I don't have $15,000 cash sitting in my account right now."
"I'm not sure this is worth the money."
"I don't know how I would pay for this."

Your job is to show them it IS possible - with the right financing plan.

Script: Introducing Financing Naturally

PATIENT SAYS
"This is more than I expected. I don't think I can afford this right now."
YOUR RESPONSE
"I totally understand - and honestly, most of our patients feel the same way at first. Here's what I want you to consider..."

"You're not paying $[total] today. With our financing options, this can be as low as $[monthly] per month. Let me break that down..."

Script: Handling Financing Concerns

OBJECTION
"I don't want to go into debt for cosmetic surgery."
RESPONSE
"I respect that - being financially responsible is important. Let me ask you this: Do you have a car payment? A phone payment? Many of us finance things we use every day."

"The difference with this is you're financing something that will make you feel confident and beautiful every single day - potentially for decades. Unlike a car that depreciates the moment you drive it off the lot, the confidence from this procedure appreciates over time."
