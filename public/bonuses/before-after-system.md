# PART 1: CAPTURING THE "BEFORE"

## Document From Day One

### The Golden Rule:
**You can't show "before" if you don't capture it.**

Start documenting from the first interaction.

---

## What To Capture (By Industry)

### For Lawyers:
- Client's emotional state at intake
- Legal situation summary
- What they stood to lose
- Previous failed attempts
- Stress and fear level
- Financial exposure

### For Dentists:
- Initial photos (with consent)
- X-rays and scans
- Pain level description
- Previous dental history
- Confidence assessment
- Smile satisfaction score

### For Coaches/Consultants:
- Starting metrics (revenue, etc.)
- Current challenges description
- Self-assessment survey
- Goals vs. reality gap
- Previous solutions tried
- Mindset evaluation

### For Realtors:
- Client's housing pain points
- Properties they couldn't find
- Budget challenges
- Timeline pressures
- Failed offers/experiences
- Dream home description

### For Service Businesses:
- Problem photos/documentation
- Customer's description of issue
- Previous fixes that failed
- Cost of the problem
- Urgency level
- Desired outcome

---

## Capture Methods

### 1. The Intake Form
Include questions like:
- "Describe your current situation"
- "What have you tried before?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate your [topic]?"
- "What is this problem costing you?"
- "What would success look like?"

### 2. The Photo/Video Documentation
- Before work begins, take photos
- Record client describing their challenge
- Screenshot current state (for digital)
- Document metrics and data points

### 3. The Baseline Survey
Create a simple 1-10 rating on:
- Problem severity
- Stress level
- Confidence
- Satisfaction
- Specific metrics

### 4. The Quote Capture
Ask and record:
- "How would you describe what you're dealing with?"
- "How does this make you feel?"
- "What happens if nothing changes?"

# PART 7: COMPLIANCE & ETHICS

## Keeping It Real And Legal

### The Truth Requirement:
- Never fabricate results
- Don't exaggerate claims
- Use real numbers only
- Include context when needed

### The Disclaimer Protocol:
For results that may vary, include:
```
"Results may vary. [Client's] experience is individual
and may not reflect typical results."
```

### The FTC Guidelines:
- Don't make false claims
- Results should be achievable
- Disclose if incentives were given
- Be prepared to substantiate claims

### Industry-Specific Rules:
- Lawyers: State bar advertising rules
- Medical: HIPAA compliance for health info
- Finance: Required disclaimers
- Check your industry regulations

---

## Ethical Before & After Practice

### DO:
- Get written consent
- Use real, accurate data
- Include relevant context
- Respect client privacy
- Show representative results

### DON'T:
- Edit photos to exaggerate
- Cherry-pick only best cases
- Imply guaranteed results
- Use without permission
- Misrepresent timelines
